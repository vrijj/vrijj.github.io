What Economists Miss¶

How often have you heard this refrain? “There aren’t even that many coalminers left to lose their jobs!”

See this tweet from Dean Baker:

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, CO has 1,300 people employed in the coal industry, 0.05 percent of statewide employment. It’s great to help people who lose jobs, but over 2 million lose their job every month. The coal transition problem is hugely exaggerated

https://twitter.com/DeanBaker13/status/1157372908374593536

But maybe people aren’t concerned about the coal miners themselves but the impact of a particularly uncaring system.If I lose my job will economists treat me the same way as they treat the coalminers? Will they ignore my plight and continue for decades to push the policies which are destroying my livelihood and then at the bleakest moment say “Well, look they’re mostly gone already so let’s just finish them off?” I actually think economists would definitely treat me that way and so do most people. You can’t make the case for an uncaring world where the professions that people build their livelihoods on can get wiped out at a moment’s notice and then somehow also expect people not to gawk at the examples that occur just because their personal livelihood isn’t staked on the exact same example.
